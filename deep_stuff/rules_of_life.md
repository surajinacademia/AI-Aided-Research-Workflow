# 10 Commandments of Life

1. Murphy's Law: "Anything that can go wrong will go wrong."

2. Muller's Theorem: "We never take credit for our best 
skills, for they come without effort and privilege."

3. Muller's Theorem: "We spend most of our effort trying to learn to do things for which we have little talent."

4. Muller's Theorem: "Everybody else's life is just as complicated as your own."

5. Uncle Ben: "With great power comes great responsibility."

6. Comparison is the key to sadness and narcissism, and academia thrives on comparison.

7. Capitalism doesn't value productivity. It values privilege and is antithetical to love, loneliness, and boredom.

8. Learning is hard, but learning from mistakes is much harder.

9. Nietzsche's Theorem: "The meaning of life is to live, is to suffer." This is the meaning of life.

10. Attachment is the root of all sadness and anxiety.

11. Dr. Colin Murray Parkes: "Grief is the price we pay for love."
